I've never understood the rampant caricaturization of 1980s film. The 1980s were a great decade for film. But "corny" and "quirky" nods to the Back II the Futures and the Terminator 2's of the decade seem like an insult to the decade's serious accomplishments, and Kung Fury is an honest attempt to be crowned king of these. It reminds me of Snow Crash, the novel by Neal Stephenson, except Snow Crash has two fanbases: One, which sees it as an angry parody of the film-and-television caricatures of cyberpunk that had become more popular since Gibson and Sterling. And two, the group of "nostalgic" fans who didn't realize it was a parody in the first place, and enjoy everything that Snow Crash puts on burn notice. Except Kung Fury has nothing to parallel the former-- It's an honest love letter to the worst things in a decade of film. I apologize for being no fun, but I wasted my time here.
